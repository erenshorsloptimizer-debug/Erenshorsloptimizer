📋 NOTES & DE-SLOPIFICATION

▶ show

What Gear Score Means — Each item's score is a single number representing how much that item contributes to your character's effectiveness for your current class and stat priorities. It is not a universal item rating — the same piece can score very differently for a Reaver versus an Arcanist. Higher score means the item does more work for your build. The score breakdown tooltip (hover any slot) shows exactly which stats are driving the number. What it doesn't tell you: score doesn't account for content context (a resistance-heavy piece may be worthless in one zone and essential in another), set bonuses if any exist, or personal playstyle preferences. Treat it as a strong starting point for comparison, not a final verdict.

How the Score is Calculated — Scoring uses the actual game formulas from Stats.cs, weighted by your stat priority tiers (Core 10 / Primary 7 / Secondary 4 / Ignore 0). Each class has different ScaleMod values baked in — STR is worth more to a Reaver (StrScaleMod 25) than an Arcanist (StrScaleMod 3) even at identical tier settings, because the game itself amplifies it less for casters. The formulas model: melee damage (STR × StrScaleMod), hit chance and crit (DEX), AC mitigation (AGI scales with level — much more valuable at level 35 than level 10), max HP (END is quadratic with level), mana pool and regen (INT and WIS), and elemental resistances (CHA). Haste uses diminishing returns near the 60% game cap. Armor Rating scores as AC × MitigationBonus², so it rewards classes with high mitigation (Paladin 1.15×) more than cloth wearers. Worn and aura effects score at full value; proc effects at half value. Blessed items multiply all stats ×1.5 and add +1 Resonance; Godly items multiply ×2 and add +2 Resonance.

Weapon DPS — Weapons are scored using the actual damage formula from Stats.cs. Melee weapons use the game's formula factor (1 + (dmg−1) × 0.4) / delay, weighted by your STR priority plus half your DEX priority; two-handed weapons get an additional 10% bonus on the STR term. Bows use flat dmg / delay weighted by DEX only — the source confirms bows have no STR or DEX scaling. Wands use flat dmg / delay weighted by INT at a reduced constant, since wand auto-attacks are a secondary damage source for casters. The score contribution is visible in the breakdown tooltip when hovering a weapon slot. What this doesn't capture: proc effects on weapons (e.g. on-hit spells), dual-wield off-hand hit probability (which is level-dependent and probabilistic), or the interaction between haste and attack delay — so treat weapon scores as a useful signal, not the full picture for weapon comparison.

Charms — Charms are included as a 15th gear slot. Unlike other slots, charms don't grant flat stats — they grant proficiency bonuses, which amplify a corresponding primary stat by 1% per point (e.g. Physicality amplifies STR, Finesse amplifies DEX). The optimizer scores charm proficiency bonuses by estimating how much each bonus point is worth given your current gear's stat totals and your stat priority weights — a Physicality bonus is worth more if you have lots of STR gear and STR is a Primary priority. Charm bonuses appear as a blue ✦ column in the Proficiency panel. Charm of the Shield is a special case: its Mitigation proficiency has no direct stat mapping, so it scores via your Armor Rating weight as a proxy — it will rank highly for tank builds (Paladin, Reaver) with Armor Rating toggled on.
